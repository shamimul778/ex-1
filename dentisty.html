What Is Cosmetic Dentistry?

Cosmetic dentistry, also known as restorative dentistry, is a type of dental practice that focuses on improving the appearance of your teeth, gums and smile. This type of dentistry can make a difference for you if you’re self-conscious about teeth that are damaged, missing, misaligned, chipped or discolored. The goal of cosmetic dentistry is to give you a smile that you won’t hesitate to show to others.

What’s The Difference Between A Cosmetic Dentist And A General Dentist?

Traditional or general dentistry is done by a general dentist or family dentist. A general dentist takes care of the health of your teeth by performing mostly preventative procedures and simple repairs. These procedures are considered necessary dental treatment. They include:

  • Teeth cleaning
  • Tooth extractions
  • Tooth fillings
  • Root canal procedures
  • Treatment of tooth pain and tooth infections

A cosmetic dentist undergoes additional training to provide care that may be considered optional or elective. This is dental treatment that you have done mainly because you want to improve the look of your teeth, although many cosmetic treatments result in health benefits as well. There’s sometimes an overlap between traditional dentistry and cosmetic dentistry, such as tooth-colored cavity fillings.
